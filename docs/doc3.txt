TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency)
The backbone of most search engines. Ranks documents by how relevant a term is — not just how often it appears, but how unique it is across the corpus. Implemented as a weighted scoring matrix in C++.
Cosine Similarity
Measures relevance between a query vector and document vectors. Converts text into numerical vectors and computes the angle between them. Higher similarity = better match.
Inverted Index
Not purely ML, but the data structure that makes ML ranking feasible at scale. Maps terms to document lists — enables O(1) lookups instead of scanning every document.
Query Expansion (NLP-based)
Uses synonym mapping or word embeddings to expand the user's query. Example: searching "fast" also retrieves results for "quick", "rapid". Improves recall significantly.
Ranking Model (Learning to Rank)
Combines multiple signals — TF-IDF score, document freshness, term position, click-through rate — into a final relevance score using a weighted linear model or a simple ML classifier.
